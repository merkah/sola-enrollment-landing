Helping more families
find SOLA

A zero-budget enrollment strategy — five concrete initiatives, no new tools, nothing about the school branding is changing.

What this is — and isn't

A set of small, concrete initiatives to help more families find SOLA online — no new tools, no new budget, and nothing that changes the school itself. Think of it as a light "tending" of the path to SOLA.

Making sure SOLA is easy to find for families who are already searching — tidying up the technical details that shape how search results see the school.

Creating something useful for families in the research phase — and a gentle way to stay connected until they're ready to take the next step.

Five layers of work

Each builds on the one before it.

The goal is to increase traffic, email signups and application submissions without compromising on quality.

What gets done

Quick wins
Foundation

Technical groundwork. One-time work with lasting effect.

  • Meta descriptions and H1 cleanup across all pages
  • Waldorf-specific keyword integration
  • Structured data and schema markup
  • Google Business Profile optimized
Capture

One strong downloadable resource, paired with a simple email signup.

  • A parent's guide to whether Waldorf is the right fit
  • Or: a visual day-in-the-life of a SOLA morning
  • Or: a Waldorf homeschool starter kit
  • Email capture + 3-part welcome sequence
  • Clear "Schedule a Visit" invitation at the right moment
Conversion

Walk the website user experience as a first-time parent. See where we can make it easy for them to make contact and stay in touch. Probably the fastest win on this list.

  • Audit the current inquiry flow end to end
  • Add a short contact form for first conversations — ask only what's needed upfront
  • Light design clean up on the Program Application page forms
  • Add "Schedule a Visit" across high-traffic pages
  • Warm confirmation email on every inquiry submitted
Long-term plan
Content

Writing that shows up when parents are searching for alternatives. Compounds over 3–6 months.

  • Deep evergreen articles on Waldorf education in Asheville
  • Shorter posts for seasonal topics and community questions
  • Targets searches like "homeschool co-ops NC," "is Waldorf right for my child"
  • 1–2 pieces per month, built to last for years
Amplification

The families already here are SOLA's most credible voices. Coordinate what happens naturally.

  • Testimonials and family stories
  • Direct, warm referral ask
  • Google review push
  • Partnerships with local parenting and homeschool networks
  • Open houses tied to the seasonal festival calendar
